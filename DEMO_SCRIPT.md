# The Deli Oracle: Hackathon Demo Script (2 Minutes)

## Theme: "Digital Play & The Vibe Engine"

### 1. The Problem (30 Seconds)
**Hook:** "New York City is a city of gatekept secrets and high-friction interactions. Finding the *real* local spots usually involves sifting through thousands of generic, sanitized reviews."
**The Play Factor:** "We wanted to take the friction of city life and turn it into **Play**. People don’t want another polite 'concierge' app; they want the raw, cynical, heart-of-gold perspective that defines New York."

### 2. The Solution: Bernie the Cat (45 Seconds)
**Introduction:** "Meet **Bernie**, a 12-year-old tabby cat who’s lived in a Bushwick bodega since the Bloomberg administration. He’s seen it all: gentrification, midnight breakups, and millions of bacon-egg-and-cheeses."
**The Interface:** "We built a high-contrast, projector-first UI. Massive typography, neon-flicker signage, and 'tabloid' styling designed to be read from the back of the room during a live pitch."
**The Tech:** "A zero-backend Next.js build using the Vercel AI SDK to stream high-personality responses from a custom-tuned LLM. Bernie speak in a heavy 70s Brooklyn accent—he’ll roast your 'fancy' coffee orders and deadass tell you where the real ones go."

### 3. The Roles & The "Shakedown" (45 Seconds)
**Role 1 (Prompt Engineering):** "We engineered the **'Shakedown Logic.'** Bernie doesn't just give advice; he funnels you to real 'Friends of the Family' like *Bushwick Gourmet Deli* or *Sey Coffee*. He’ll give you a code like `CAT-PICKLE-24` and tell you the owner 'owes him tuna.'"
**Role 3 (Projector UI):** "Optimized for high-impact visibility. Using `vw` units and midnight-black contrast to ensure Bernie’s judgment is legible from 30 feet away."
**Role 4 (Vibe & Interactivity):** "Implemented the 'Bodega Doorbell' chime and breathing cat visuals to make the interaction feel tactile and alive."

### 4. Closing
"The Deli Oracle isn't a logistics tool—it's a vibe engine. We aren't just giving you a map; we're giving you a character. Because in this city, if you aren't playing, you're just commuting. Tell 'em Bernie sent ya!"
